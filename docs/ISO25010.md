# ISO/IEC 25010 Product Quality Evaluation

System: Cybersecurity Integrated Digital Leave Management System (LGU Alicia).
This document evaluates the software against the eight ISO/IEC 25010 product-quality
characteristics, cites the implementing evidence, and provides the survey instrument used
for the thesis acceptance evaluation.

## 1. Characteristic evaluation

| Characteristic | Sub-characteristics addressed | Evidence in this system | Rating* |
|----------------|-------------------------------|-------------------------|---------|
| **Functional Suitability** | completeness, correctness, appropriateness | All manuscript FRs implemented (Requirements.md); CSC Form 6 + 15 leave types; approval workflow; credit computation verified by `LeaveWorkflowTest`, `LeaveCreditServiceTest` | Excellent |
| **Performance Efficiency** | time-behaviour, resource use, capacity | DB indexes on hot paths; pagination on every list; Redis/DB cache; queued mail/notifications; eager-loading avoids N+1 | Very Good |
| **Compatibility** | co-existence, interoperability | REST API `/api/v1` + OpenAPI; standard MySQL; runs alongside other XAMPP apps; exports to PDF/XLSX/CSV | Very Good |
| **Usability** | learnability, operability, accessibility, UI aesthetics, error protection | Bootstrap 5 government UI, dark/light, responsive, skeleton loaders, SweetAlert toasts; inline validation; conditional CSC form; labelled/`:focus-visible` accessible controls | Excellent |
| **Reliability** | maturity, availability, fault tolerance, recoverability | DB transactions + row locks on balance updates (no negative balance, concurrency-safe test); append-only audit; backup/restore commands; graceful error pages | Excellent |
| **Security** | confidentiality, integrity, non-repudiation, accountability, authenticity | Full STRIDE (ThreatModel.md), OTP MFA, RBAC, IDS, audit trail, encryption of session/cookies, lockout; verified by 17 security/auth/RBAC tests | Excellent |
| **Maintainability** | modularity, reusability, analysability, modifiability, testability | Service + Repository-style layering, SOLID, PSR-12, DI container, ADRs; 47 automated tests; JSON-configurable leave policies (no migration for new types) | Excellent |
| **Portability** | adaptability, installability, replaceability | Engine-portable migrations (MySQL/SQLite); vendored offline assets; `.env` config; XAMPP install guide; seeders/factories | Very Good |

\* Ratings are the developer self-assessment; the survey below produces the respondent-based scores for the thesis defense.

## 3. Evaluation survey instrument (ISO 25010, 5-point Likert)

Respondents (HR officers, department heads, employees, IT admin) rate 1 = Strongly Disagree … 5 = Strongly Agree.

**Functional Suitability**
1. The system provides all the leave-processing functions I need.
2. Leave credits and balances are computed correctly.
3. The digital CSC Form 6 matches the official form.

**Performance Efficiency**
4. Pages and reports load quickly.
5. The system stays responsive with many records.

**Compatibility**
6. Exports (PDF/Excel/CSV) open correctly in my other tools.

**Usability**
7. The system is easy to learn and navigate.
8. Messages and warnings are clear and helpful.
9. The interface is readable (including dark mode) and accessible.

**Reliability**
10. The system behaves consistently without errors or lost data.
11. I trust the approval and balance records to be accurate.

**Security**
12. Login, OTP and account protections make the system feel secure.
13. I am confident actions are logged and traceable.

**Maintainability** (IT staff)
14. The system is straightforward to configure and administer.

**Portability** (IT staff)
15. The system was straightforward to install and set up.

### Scoring
Per-characteristic mean = average of its items; overall mean = average of all items.
Interpretation: 4.21–5.00 Excellent, 3.41–4.20 Very Good, 2.61–3.40 Good, 1.81–2.60 Fair, 1.00–1.80 Poor.
Record N, per-item mean, SD, and per-characteristic mean in the thesis results chapter.
